The action comes as lawmakers and administration officials are increasingly voicing concern that Russia may be using cryptocurrency to avoid the impact of sanctions imposed on its banks, oligarchs and oil industry due to the invasion of Ukraine. Last week, Democratic Senators. Elizabeth Warren, Mark Warner, and Jack Reed asked the Treasury Department to provide information on how it intends to inhibit cryptocurrency use for sanctions evasion. The Biden administration has argued that Russia won’t be able to make up for the loss of U.S. and European business by turning to cryptocurrency. Officials said the Democratic President's order had been in the works for months before Russia's Vladimir Putin invaded Ukraine last month. Daleep Singh, a deputy national security and economic adviser to Mr. Biden, told CNN on Wednesday that “crypto’s really not a workaround for our sanctions.”
